Section 5~12%

Optimizing Performance & Cost

Apply FinOps and recommenders to trim spend without hurting reliability.

FinOps levers & recommenders

Use Active Assist and Google Cloud recommenders (cost, security, performance, reliability, manageability) for right-sizing and waste removal. Apply Spot VMs for fault-tolerant work, committed-use and sustained-use discounts for steady load, and choose appropriate network tiers.

Per-workload optimisation

Tune individual workloads: GKE bin-packing and autoscaling, Cloud Run concurrency/min-instances, and Compute Engine right-sizing. Manage observability cost itself with log exclusions and metric sampling.

Cost visibility & accountability

You can't optimise what you can't see. Use billing export to BigQuery for detailed analysis, labels on resources for cost allocation/showback, and budgets and alerts to catch overruns early. The FinOps loop is inform → optimise → operate: attribute spend, act on recommenders, then make it a habit.

⚠️ Exam Trap: "Attribute cost to each team/service" relies on consistent resource labels + billing export, not eyeballing the console billing page.
FinOps Inform (export + labels), Optimise (recommenders + discounts), Operate (budgets + habits).

🧠 Warm-Up Quiz

Q1. Find right-sizing and idle-resource savings recommendations across the project. What do you use?

Reveal answer

Active Assist / Google Cloud recommenders.

Surface cost, performance, and reliability recommendations.

Q2. Reduce compute cost for a fault-tolerant nightly batch. Which option?

Reveal answer

Spot VMs.

Deep discount for preemptible, restartable workloads.

Q3. Finance wants per-team cost attribution and early warning on overruns. What do you set up?

Reveal answer

Resource labels + billing export to BigQuery, plus budgets and alerts.

Labels attribute spend; export enables analysis; budgets alert before overrun.

Q4. A steady 24/7 production fleet of predictable size needs cheaper compute without interruption risk. Which discount?

Reveal answer

Committed-use discounts.

Best for steady, predictable load; Spot risks preemption, so it's wrong for steady prod.

📝 Exam-Style Practice

Q1. A nightly batch pipeline is fault-tolerant and restartable, and finance wants its compute cost minimised. Which option fits?

  • A) Committed-use discounts on always-on standard VMs.
  • B) Spot VMs, with the job designed to resume after preemption.
  • C) A larger machine type to finish the batch faster.
  • D) Premium network tier to speed the job.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Spot VMs are deeply discounted and ideal for interruptible, restartable batch — exactly this workload.

A: CUDs suit steady 24/7 load, not a nightly job. C: A bigger machine may cost more, not less. D: Network tier doesn't address compute cost for batch.

Q2. Leadership wants each team's cloud spend attributed back to them and an alert before any team blows its monthly budget. What should the platform team implement?

  • A) A monthly manual export of the console billing summary into a slide deck.
  • B) Consistent resource labels plus billing export to BigQuery, with budgets and alerts per team.
  • C) Separate billing accounts created ad hoc when teams ask.
  • D) IAM restrictions so only finance can view billing.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Labels attribute spend, billing export enables per-team analysis, and budgets/alerts catch overruns early — the standard FinOps showback setup.

A: Manual decks are stale and unattributed. C: Ad-hoc billing accounts fragment governance. D: Hiding billing doesn't attribute or alert on cost.

Q3. A platform engineer wants a single place to surface right-sizing, idle-resource, and committed-use savings opportunities across the whole project. What should they consult?

  • A) Active Assist / Google Cloud recommenders.
  • B) The raw billing CSV for last month.
  • C) Cloud Trace latency reports.
  • D) The IAM Recommender only.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: A. Active Assist recommenders surface cost (right-sizing, idle, CUD), performance, security, and reliability recommendations in one place.

B: A billing CSV shows spend, not actionable recommendations. C: Trace is for latency, not cost. D: The IAM Recommender is one narrow recommender, not the full cost set.
