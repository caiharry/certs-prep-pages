Section 6~13%

Monitoring AI Solutions

Identify risk, apply Responsible AI, and distinguish skew from drift.

Skew vs drift

  • Training-serving skew — training and serving data or preprocessing differ; fix by sharing preprocessing / a Feature Store.
  • Prediction (feature) drift — the serving input distribution shifts over time versus training; detect with Vertex AI Model Monitoring.
  • Feature attribution drift — the relative importance of features changes over time, hinting the world has moved.
  • Concept drift — the relationship between inputs and the label itself changes (the right answer moves).
⚠️ Exam Trap: Good offline metrics but poor production results from day one = training-serving skew. Metrics that decay over weeks = drift. The timing tells you which.

Setting up Model Monitoring

Vertex AI Model Monitoring compares live serving inputs (and optionally attributions) against a baseline — usually the training distribution — and raises alerts when divergence crosses a threshold you set. Sample a fraction of traffic to control cost, route alerts to Cloud Monitoring, and feed them into the retraining trigger you built in the pipelines section.

BASE No baseline, no monitoring. Drift is always measured relative to the training distribution.

Responsible AI & explainability

Check for bias and fairness across slices before launch, assess solution readiness, and use Vertex Explainable AI (feature attributions, e.g. integrated gradients / sampled Shapley) so each decision can be explained — essential in regulated domains like lending or healthcare. Attribution drift also doubles as an early-warning signal.

⚠️ Exam Trap: "We must justify each individual decision to a regulator" = Explainable AI per-prediction attributions, not aggregate accuracy or a confusion matrix.

Securing models & GenAI safety

Protect models and data from exploitation: restrict endpoint access with IAM and private networking, guard against data exfiltration with VPC Service Controls, and for GenAI watch for prompt injection and unsafe output. Apply safety filters and human-in-the-loop review for high-risk actions, and log prompts/responses for audit.

GUARD Least-privilege access + private serving + safety filters = a model that's hard to abuse.

🧠 Warm-Up Quiz

Q1. Production accuracy drops as incoming feature distributions shift. What detects this?

Reveal answer

Vertex AI Model Monitoring (feature drift).

Compares serving inputs against a baseline and alerts on divergence.

Q2. A lending model must explain which features drove each decision. Which capability?

Reveal answer

Vertex Explainable AI feature attributions.

Produces per-prediction attributions for transparency.

Q3. A model is great in tests but underperforms from the very first day in production. Skew or drift?

Reveal answer

Training-serving skew.

Day-one gaps point to a training/serving mismatch, not a gradual distribution shift over time.

Q4. What must Model Monitoring be given before it can detect drift?

Reveal answer

A baseline distribution (usually the training data).

Drift is divergence from that baseline, so it's required to compare against.

📝 Exam-Style Practice

Q1. A model scored well on the holdout set but produced noticeably worse results the moment it went live, before any data could have shifted. What is the most likely cause?

  • A) Concept drift caused by a changing input-label relationship.
  • B) Training-serving skew from differing preprocessing or features.
  • C) Gradual feature drift accumulating over several weeks.
  • D) Overfitting that only appears after months of serving.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. A gap present from day one, with no time for distributions to move, is training-serving skew — the serving path differs from training.

A/C: Drift develops over time; it can't explain an immediate launch-day gap. D: Overfitting shows up in evaluation, not specifically at the train/serve boundary.

Q2. A regulated lender must justify each individual loan decision the model makes. Which capability satisfies the requirement?

  • A) An aggregate accuracy and confusion-matrix report per quarter.
  • B) Vertex AI Model Monitoring drift alerts on the input features.
  • C) Vertex Explainable AI per-prediction feature attributions.
  • D) A traffic split that A/B tests two model versions.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: C. Per-prediction feature attributions explain why this decision was made — exactly what a regulator demands for individual cases.

A: Aggregate metrics don't explain a single decision. B: Drift detection watches distributions, not individual rationale. D: A/B testing compares versions, not decisions.

Q3. You want production retraining to fire automatically when the live feature distribution diverges from training, with cost kept reasonable. What should you set up?

  • A) Model Monitoring on a sample of traffic, with a baseline and a drift threshold that triggers the pipeline.
  • B) A fixed daily retraining job regardless of whether anything changed.
  • C) Manual quarterly reviews of accuracy dashboards.
  • D) Explainable AI attributions logged but not alerted on.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: A. Monitoring a sampled stream against a baseline detects drift cheaply, and the threshold alert triggers retraining only when needed.

B: Daily retraining wastes compute when nothing changed. C: Quarterly manual review is slow and misses fast shifts. D: Logging attributions without alerts won't trigger anything.
