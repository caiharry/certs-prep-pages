Section 5~11%

Supporting Compliance

Map regulatory and industry requirements onto Google Cloud controls — a small, high-yield slice.

Shared responsibility & regionalisation

Know the shared responsibility model (Google secures the infrastructure; you secure configuration, identity, and data). Enforce data residency with Assured Workloads and the resource-location org policy. Use Access Transparency (logs of Google access) and Access Approval (you approve Google access) for regulated estates.

Mapping compliance to controls

Translate requirements (e.g., SOC 2, PCI, data sovereignty) into concrete controls: network/access segmentation, audit-log coverage, encryption with CMEK, and least-privilege IAM. Determine which parts of the environment are in scope for each regulation.

Transparency vs Approval vs Assured Workloads

Three controls cluster around regulated estates and the exam tests the difference. Access Transparency gives you near-real-time logs of when Google personnel access your content (e.g. during a support case) — visibility only. Access Approval adds a gate: Google must request, and you must explicitly approve, before that access happens. Assured Workloads is broader still — it creates a compliance-boundary folder that enforces data residency, personnel controls (support staff location/citizenship), and a curated set of services for regimes like FedRAMP, CJIS, or EU sovereignty.

  • Access Transparency — see Google access after the fact (logs).
  • Access Approval — approve Google access before it happens (gate).
  • Assured Workloads — enforce residency, personnel, and service constraints (boundary).
⚠️ Exam Trap: "We need a log of Google access" = Access Transparency. "We must approve it first" = Access Approval. Don't confuse the passive log with the active gate.

Enforcing residency & auditing for frameworks

Beyond Assured Workloads, the day-to-day residency guardrail is the resource-location org policy (constraints/gcp.resourceLocations), which stops resources being created outside approved regions. To demonstrate compliance, lean on evidence: Cloud Audit Logs for who-did-what, Security Command Center compliance reports mapping findings to standards (CIS, PCI DSS, NIST), and CMEK so you control the keys. Decide which projects are in scope for each framework and isolate them with the resource hierarchy and VPC Service Controls so the audit surface stays small.

  • Resource-location org policy — block resource creation outside approved regions.
  • SCC compliance dashboard — map posture to CIS/PCI/NIST benchmarks.
  • Scoping — isolate in-scope projects to shrink the audit boundary.
SEE / GATE / BOUND Transparency lets you see, Approval is the gate, Assured Workloads draws the boundary.

🧠 Warm-Up Quiz

Q1. Regulated workload must run only in EU regions with controls to support compliance. Which service?

Reveal answer

Assured Workloads.

Enforces data residency, personnel, and control constraints for compliance regimes.

Q2. You must approve any Google support access to your data. Which feature?

Reveal answer

Access Approval (with Access Transparency).

Requires explicit customer approval and logs Google access.

Q3. You only need a log showing when Google staff accessed your content during support, with no approval workflow. Which feature?

Reveal answer

Access Transparency.

It records Google administrative access to your content as logs, without gating it.

Q4. Prevent developers from creating any resource outside approved regions, without buying a full compliance program. Which control?

Reveal answer

The resource-location organization policy (constraints/gcp.resourceLocations).

It restricts where resources can be created and inherits across the hierarchy.

📝 Exam-Style Practice

Q1. A government customer's regulation requires that any Google support engineer who touches their data must be located in-country, that data stays in approved regions, and that only an approved service set is used. Which offering meets all three at once?

  • A) Access Approval combined with the resource-location organization policy
  • B) Assured Workloads configured for the relevant compliance regime
  • C) A VPC Service Controls perimeter plus CMEK on all data services
  • D) Access Transparency logs reviewed alongside Security Command Center findings
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Assured Workloads is the single offering that bundles personnel controls (support-staff location), data residency, and a restricted approved-service set for a chosen compliance regime.

A: This pair covers approval and residency but not the support-personnel location requirement. C: VPC-SC and CMEK protect data exfiltration and keys but say nothing about where support staff are located or residency enforcement. D: These provide visibility into access and posture, not enforcement of residency, personnel, or service constraints.

Q2. An auditor for a PCI assessment asks you to demonstrate that your in-scope projects are continuously checked against an industry benchmark and to produce evidence of the findings. Which approach gives this with the least custom build?

  • A) Export all audit logs to BigQuery and write SQL that re-implements the benchmark checks
  • B) Schedule a third-party scanner against each project and collate its reports manually
  • C) Use the Security Command Center compliance dashboard mapped to the relevant benchmark
  • D) Enable Access Transparency and present the Google-access logs as compliance evidence
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: C. Security Command Center maps Security Health Analytics findings to industry benchmarks such as PCI DSS and CIS out of the box, providing continuous checks and exportable evidence with minimal build.

A: Re-implementing benchmark logic in SQL is exactly the heavy custom build the question asks you to avoid. B: Manual collation of third-party reports is neither continuous nor low-effort. D: Access Transparency logs Google access; it is unrelated to benchmarking your configuration against PCI.

Q3. A data-sovereignty rule says certain workloads must never have resources created outside two approved European regions, and the restriction must hold across all current and future projects without depending on per-project setup. What enforces this?

  • A) Setting the resource-location organization policy at the org node listing the two approved regions
  • B) A hierarchical firewall policy permitting only traffic to and from the two regions
  • C) Tagging resources by region and auditing the tags with a scheduled report
  • D) A VPC Service Controls perimeter restricted to subnets in the two approved regions
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: A. The gcp.resourceLocations org policy set at the org node inherits to all current and future projects and blocks resource creation outside the listed regions — preventive, not detective.

B: Firewall policies govern network traffic, not where resources may be created. C: Tagging and reporting only detects violations after the fact and relies on per-resource discipline. D: VPC-SC limits API data movement across a perimeter; it does not constrain the regions in which resources are provisioned.
