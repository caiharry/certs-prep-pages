Section 4~15%

Analyzing & Optimizing Processes

Technical and business processes: SDLC and CI/CD, disaster recovery, change management, and cost optimization.

Disaster-recovery tiers

Trade cost against RTO (time to recover) and RPO (acceptable data loss):

  • Backup & restore — cheapest, hours of RTO.
  • Cold standby — rebuild from IaC after outage.
  • Warm standby — scaled-down replica promoted in minutes.
  • Hot / active-active — near-zero RTO/RPO, priciest.
⚠️ Exam Trap: Match the tier to the stated RTO/RPO and budget. A ~10-minute RTO with small data loss and moderate cost points to warm standby, not active-active.

Cost optimization levers

Use committed-use discounts (resource commitments for steady workloads) and automatic sustained-use discounts; Spot VMs for fault-tolerant batch; right-size via Active Assist / Recommender; apply storage lifecycle policies and autoscaling. Cloud shifts spend from CapEx to OpEx — a common business-process answer.

CUD Committed-use discounts are commitments on resources (vCPU/RAM), not specific VMs — they survive resizing.

SDLC, CI/CD & change management

Optimise the delivery process, not just the infrastructure. Automate the path to prod with Cloud Build (CI), Artifact Registry (artifacts), and Cloud Deploy (managed progressive delivery with approvals across environments). Track the DORA metrics (deployment frequency, lead time, change-failure rate, MTTR) and gate risky changes with approvals and automated rollback rather than change-advisory-board bottlenecks.

⚠️ Exam Trap: "Promote a release through dev→staging→prod with approvals and easy rollback" points to Cloud Deploy, not hand-rolled scripts.

🧠 Warm-Up Quiz

Q1. A service must recover within ~10 minutes with only a few minutes of data loss, at moderate cost. Which DR pattern?

Reveal answer

Warm standby.

A scaled-down running replica with replication promotes within minutes at moderate cost.

Q2. Steady 24/7 workload, predictable size. Best discount mechanism?

Reveal answer

Committed-use discounts.

CUDs reward steady resource usage; Spot is for fault-tolerant batch, not steady production.

Q3. You need managed promotion of a release across dev, staging, and prod with approvals and one-click rollback. Which service?

Reveal answer

Cloud Deploy.

It's the managed continuous-delivery service with pipelines, approvals, and rollback across targets.

Q4. You resize committed VMs frequently. Do committed-use discounts still apply?

Reveal answer

Yes — CUDs commit to resources (vCPU/RAM), not specific VMs.

The discount applies to the committed amount of resource usage regardless of which VMs consume it.

📝 Exam-Style Practice

Q1. A line-of-business app must come back within roughly 10 minutes of a regional failure, can lose at most a few minutes of data, and the budget is moderate — not unlimited. Which DR pattern fits best?

  • A) Backup and restore from nightly snapshots.
  • B) A warm standby: a scaled-down replica kept in sync, promoted on failover.
  • C) A multi-region active-active deployment.
  • D) A cold standby rebuilt from IaC after the outage.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. A warm standby promotes a running, replicating replica within minutes and only loses recent data — matching the ~10-minute RTO, small RPO, and moderate budget.

A/D: Backup-restore and cold standby take far longer than 10 minutes. C: Active-active meets the RTO but costs the most, beyond a moderate budget.

Q2. A steady, 24/7 production workload of predictable size runs on Compute Engine and finance wants to cut its bill. Which mechanism gives the best savings without risking interruption?

  • A) Move the workload to Spot VMs.
  • B) Purchase committed-use discounts for the steady resource usage.
  • C) Rely solely on automatic sustained-use discounts.
  • D) Schedule the VMs to stop overnight.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. CUDs give the deepest discount for steady, predictable usage and never interrupt the workload.

A: Spot VMs can be preempted — unsafe for steady production. C: Sustained-use discounts help but are smaller than a commitment. D: A 24/7 workload can't be stopped overnight.

Q3. Leadership wants releases promoted automatically through dev, staging, and production, each stage gated by approval, with fast rollback on failure. Which Google Cloud service should anchor this?

  • A) Cloud Build alone, triggering deploys with custom scripts.
  • B) Cloud Deploy delivery pipelines with targets, approvals, and rollback.
  • C) A Cloud Scheduler job that runs deployment commands nightly.
  • D) Manual gcloud deploys performed by the on-call engineer.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Cloud Deploy is the managed CD service with multi-stage pipelines, approvals, and built-in rollback — exactly the workflow described.

A: Cloud Build does CI; promotion/approvals/rollback are Cloud Deploy's job. C/D: Cron scripts and manual deploys lack approvals and reliable rollback.
