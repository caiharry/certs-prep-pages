Section 3~18%

Site Reliability Engineering

Balance change velocity and reliability with SLIs, SLOs, and error budgets.

SLIs, SLOs, SLAs & error budgets

An SLI measures a quality (availability, latency); an SLO is your internal target for it; an SLA is the external contract (with penalties). The error budget = 1 − SLO; when it's exhausted, freeze risky launches and prioritise reliability.

⚠️ Exam Trap: Out of error budget? Defer new features — don't ship. SLOs are internal; never quietly change the customer SLA to dodge a low budget.
Nines Each extra "nine" of availability costs exponentially more — match the target to the business need.

Lifecycle, capacity & incident mitigation

Plan capacity with quotas, reservations, and Dynamic Workload Scheduler; autoscale with MIGs, Cloud Run, and GKE. Mitigate incident impact by draining/redirecting traffic, adding capacity, and rolling back.

Toil, postmortems & alerting

SRE attacks toil (manual, repetitive, automatable work) so engineers spend time on engineering, not firefighting. After incidents, run blameless postmortems that fix systems, not people. Alert on symptoms that burn the error budget (SLO burn-rate alerts), not on every low-level cause — this cuts alert fatigue and pages humans only when users are actually affected.

⚠️ Exam Trap: Page on user-facing symptoms / SLO burn rate, not on raw CPU spikes that may not affect users — cause-based alerts create noise.
TOIL If it's manual and repetitive, automate it; if it broke, write a blameless postmortem; if it pages, make sure a user actually cared.

🧠 Warm-Up Quiz

Q1. You served 99.00% against a 99% SLA and the error budget is spent. What's the SRE move on a new feature?

Reveal answer

Defer the deployment until budget recovers.

No budget remaining means prioritise stability over change.

Q2. Difference between an SLO and an SLA?

Reveal answer

SLO is the internal target; SLA is the external contract with penalties.

SLOs guide engineering; SLAs are customer commitments.

Q3. On-call is drowning in pages from CPU spikes that don't affect users. What should alerts target instead?

Reveal answer

User-facing symptoms / SLO burn rate.

Alerting on budget-burning symptoms pages only when users are impacted, cutting noise.

Q4. After a major outage, what kind of review does SRE prescribe?

Reveal answer

A blameless postmortem.

It fixes systemic causes without blaming individuals, encouraging honest disclosure.

📝 Exam-Style Practice

Q1. A service has exhausted its monthly error budget against a 99.9% SLO, yet the product team wants to ship a risky new feature. What does SRE practice prescribe?

  • A) Ship the feature anyway and quietly lower the SLA to stay compliant.
  • B) Freeze risky releases and prioritise reliability work until the budget recovers.
  • C) Raise the SLO to 100% so there's no budget pressure.
  • D) Remove the SLO so deployments aren't blocked.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. An empty error budget means slow down on change and invest in reliability — that's the whole point of the budget.

A: Quietly changing the customer SLA is dishonest and unrelated to the internal SLO. C/D: Inflating or removing the SLO games the metric instead of improving reliability.

Q2. On-call engineers are burning out from frequent pages triggered by short CPU and memory spikes that rarely correlate with user-visible problems. How should the team redesign alerting?

  • A) Raise the CPU alert thresholds slightly and keep paging on them.
  • B) Alert on SLO burn rate and user-facing symptoms rather than low-level causes.
  • C) Route all alerts to email so nobody is paged.
  • D) Add more granular per-resource alerts for completeness.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Symptom/SLO-burn-rate alerting pages only when users are actually affected, eliminating cause-based noise and fatigue.

A: Higher thresholds still page on non-impacting causes. C: Email-only risks missing real incidents. D: More cause-based alerts worsen the noise.

Q3. A team spends hours each week manually restarting a service and clearing queues after recurring failures. What is the SRE-aligned response?

  • A) Accept the manual work as normal operations and add more on-call staff.
  • B) Treat it as toil: automate the remediation and fix the underlying cause via a postmortem.
  • C) Schedule the restarts more frequently to pre-empt failures.
  • D) Increase the SLA penalty to motivate the team.
Reveal answer & explanation

Correct: B. Repetitive manual remediation is toil; SRE automates it and drives out the root cause through a blameless postmortem.

A: Throwing people at toil doesn't scale. C: More frequent restarts treat the symptom, not the cause. D: SLA penalties don't fix the engineering problem.
